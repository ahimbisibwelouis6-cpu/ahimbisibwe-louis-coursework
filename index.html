The Moon

The Moon is Earth's only natural satellite and the fifth largest moon in the solar system. It plays a major role in controlling ocean tides and stabilizing Earth's rotation. The Moon does not produce its own light; it reflects sunlight, which makes it visible at night. Its surface is covered with craters formed by asteroid impacts over billions of years.
